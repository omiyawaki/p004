### **Summary of Key Points: Seasonal Cycle of Amplified Hot Day Warming**

(Based on analysis of 2023-09-27 CAS Meeting Slides)

1.  Phenomenon: Hot days over land warm more than average days
    (amplified warming). This amplification has a strong seasonal cycle
    often as large as the annual mean signal itself. Focusing on the
    annual maximum amplification reveals regional hotspots missed by the
    annual mean view.

2.  Spatio-Temporal Patterns: Peak amplified warming generally occurs
    during local summer over most continental areas. This is
    particularly robust in the tropics and high latitudes. Mid-latitude
    patterns are more complex. Winter months often show suppressed
    amplification especially in high latitudes.

3.  Mechanism 1: Flux Repartitioning: The primary mechanism explored is
    the repartitioning of surface turbulent fluxes. On hot days
    decreased latent heat flux (evaporative cooling) is compensated by
    increased sensible heat flux leading to higher surface temperatures.
    This mechanism aligns well with the observed seasonality
    particularly in the tropics. It appears less consistent in
    mid-latitudes and does not explain the high-latitude patterns well.

4.  Mechanism 2: Soil Moisture Control: The decrease in latent heat flux
    is primarily driven by changes in soil moisture (SM). This
    relationship can be analyzed using frameworks like the Budyko curve
    or piecewise functions fitted to LH-SM data which account for
    different moisture regimes (dry transitional wet active rain).

5.  Mechanism 3: Pathways: The contribution of soil moisture changes
    (ΔSM) to the LH decrease can be further understood through two main
    pathways:

    - Pathway 1: Occurs when mean days are energy-limited but hot days
      become water-limited leading to high sensitivity of LH to SM
      changes.

    - Pathway 2: Occurs when both mean and hot days are water-limited
      but the *decrease* in SM is amplified on hot days.

6.  Pathway Dominance: The relative importance of these pathways varies
    spatially and seasonally. Analysis suggests:

    - Pathway 1 is dominant in South America.

    - Pathway 2 is dominant in the Sahel.

    - Both pathways contribute significantly in Southern Africa and
      Southeast Asia.

    - Pathway 1 tends to be more important earlier in the warm season
      while Pathway 2 dominates later.

7.  Outstanding Issues: The mechanisms driving amplified warming in high
    latitudes remain less clear as the soil moisture and flux
    repartitioning explanations seem less applicable there.
