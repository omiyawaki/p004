Motivation

- Daily temperature variability has important economic and health
  implications (Kotz et al. 2024)

  - Agricultural productivity (Zhu et al. 2022)

  - Physiological limit to heat stress (Sherwood and Huber 2010)

- Spatial variability matters

  - Impacts on economic productivity is also spatially heterogeneous

    - Largest decrease in low latitudes

    - Increase in high latitudes

Overview of literature

- TROPICS

  - Tropical dynamics constraint

    - Moist adiabatic adjustment and weak temperature gradient
      assumption leads to strong constraint on surface mse over land
      (Zhang and Fueglistaler 2020, Zhang et al. 2021, Byrne 2021)

      - Does this work across percentiles?

      - Does this explain spatial patterns?

  - Land atmosphere feedbacks

    - Repartitioning of surface turbulent fluxes to sensible cooling
      leads to increase in near surface air temperature (Duan et al.
      2020)

      - Works spatially

    - Soil moisture change contributes to latent cooling change (Koster
      et al. 2004, Seneviratne 2010, Hsu and Dirmeyer 2022, Hsu and
      Dirmeyer 2023)

- EXTRATROPICS

- Amplified cold day warming in mid and high latitudes in fall, winter,
  spring is known

  - Gross et al. (2020)

- Temperature advection

  - Temperature advection is important for daily temperature variability
    in climatology (i.e., not CC)

    - Screen (2014), Garfinkel and Harnik (2017)

  - Temperature advection is important for temperature variance in
    extratropics but not the tropics

    - Linz et al. (2020), Zhang et al. (2022)

  - Temperature advection important for temperature variability across
    daily to monthly timescales

    - Schneider et al. (2015), Holmes et al. (2016)

  - Temperature advection shown to be important for high latitude
    extreme temperature response

    - Screen (2014), Schneider et al (2015), Gross et al (2020)

  - Temperature advection mechanism relies on Arctic Amplification

    - Sea ice loss simulations show cold days become less frequent with
      warming, consistent with the importance of sea ice loss on Arctic
      Amplification

      - Screen et al. (2015)

- SNOW

  - Snow can both amplify (e.g., albedo) and mute (latent heat) warming

    - Cohen and Rind (1990)

  - Snowmelt amplifies cold day warming in NH autumn and winter

    - Gross et al. (2020)

- However, some studies argue cold extremes become colder, especially in
  recent records

  - E.g., Tang et al. (2013), Cohen et al. (2014)

Haghighi et al. (2018). Multidimensional BC framework

- Wind speed and air temperature important for spread in EF-SM
  relationship

- Limitation: bare soil only. Doesn’t consider transpiration

Holmes et al. (2016). **Seasonality of temperature variability**

- Focus on *monthly* variability

- In winter, variability in midlatitudes decreases whereas in very high
  latitudes and the tropics it increases.

- In summer, variability increases over most land areas and in the
  tropics, with decreasing variability in high latitude oceans.

- Temperature advection is found to be of particular importance in the
  Northern Hemisphere winter midlatitudes, where the
  <span class="mark">change in mean state temperature gradients alone
  could account for over half the projected changes</span>.

Rothlisberger and Papritz (2023): backtrajectory analysis of annual
coldest day

- Focuses on annual coldest day (i.e., wintertime coldest days)

- Focuses on physical processes that influence cold days in
  **climatology** (not climate change)

- Advection dominates over the ocean

- Both advection and diabatic cooling contribute over land
