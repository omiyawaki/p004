1) Key Narrative Claims
- Seasonal hot-day amplification in the tropics is strongly phase-locked: NH peaks in boreal summer, SH in austral summer, and combined tropics peak mid-year (slides 2-6).
- The deck argues reduced hot-day latent cooling (`ΔLH^95 < 0`) co-occurs with stronger hot-day warming, especially over land hotspots (slides 14-15, 21).
- Wind-speed changes are tested as a mechanism but presented as mostly not explanatory globally, with limited regional exceptions (Sahel, parts of central US; slides 8-10 plus notes text).
- Relative-humidity changes are likewise tested; reduced RH often co-occurs with reduced hot-day LH, but causality is presented as conditional/regional (slides 11-13 plus notes text).
- CESM2 hot-day LH anomalies from ESGF `hfls` and Glade `LHFLX` are shown as near-equivalent, supporting variable consistency for this diagnostic (slide 16).
- LH component decomposition suggests transpiration (`QVEGT`) contributes materially to hot-day latent-cooling reductions, while evaporation terms show mixed behavior (slides 17-20 plus notes text).

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slides 2-3: line plots of `ΔδT^95` show NH tropical maximum around Jun-Aug and SH maxima near Nov-Jan; combined-tropics peak around Jul.
- Slides 4-7: gridpoint-by-month heatmaps show a clear diagonal seasonal structure and concentration of positive anomalies in warm-season months.
- Slides 8-10: paired maps compare reduced hot-day LH against enhanced winds and mean/hot wind anomalies; spatial overlap appears limited outside select regions.
- Slides 11-13: analogous RH diagnostics show broad RH decreases where hot-day LH decreases, with stronger large-scale coherence than wind maps.
- Slides 14-15: CESM2 and MMM enhanced hot-day warming maps align with reduced hot-day LH regions (e.g., parts of South America, northern high latitudes).
- Slide 21: reduced hot-day evaporative fraction map includes heavy stippling/noisy high-latitude structure, indicating large uncertainty or masking artifacts there.

- Frame this meeting as a mechanism-check section: hot-day warming amplification linked to reduced latent cooling, then stress-tests against wind, RH, and ET components.
