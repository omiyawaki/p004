# Meeting Notes: 2023-11-03

## Key Narrative Claims

- The core claim is that hot-extreme warming structure is organized by seasonality and hydroclimate regime, not only by mean-state warming.
- Visual progression from global maps to AI-percentile/month heatmaps suggests that high-latitude behavior is distinct from tropical and midlatitude behavior, with stronger sign reversals across parts of the annual cycle.
- A second central claim is process attribution: changes in latent heat (`LH`) and sensible heat (`SH`) partitioning depend on movement across water-limited vs energy-limited regimes (conceptual framework in Fig. 4), with multiple pathway decompositions.
- The "month sorted by increasing AI" sequence indicates that ordering by aridity can expose cleaner structure than strict calendar ordering for some diagnostics.
- Supplementary regional panels imply heterogeneity within each broad latitude band (Africa, Australia, Asia, Europe, North America, South America), including sparse/hatched regions that should not be overinterpreted.

## Figure-Level Highlights

- **Fig. 1**: Four global maps (annual mean, seasonal amplitude, annual max, annual min) for `\Delta \delta T^{95}` indicate stronger positive anomalies in annual maximum/seasonal amplitude over many land regions, while annual minima show large negative anomalies at high latitudes.
- **Fig. 2**: Month-of-year timing map plus banded AI-percentile heatmaps show tropical warming mostly positive, midlatitudes mixed/weakly negative in many bins, and high latitudes with strong negative values over large month-percentile domains plus isolated positive pockets.
- **Fig. 3**: `\Delta \delta SH^{95}` and `\Delta \delta LH^{95}` panels by latitude band show complementary behavior: SH often increases where LH decreases, consistent with altered evaporative partitioning.
- **Fig. 4**: Conceptual regime diagram frames mechanism: as soil moisture (`SM`) shifts, hot-day states can move along different pathways with distinct `LH` responses; ET-regime histogram shows concentration in mid AI percentiles.
- **Fig. 5**: Decomposition (`BC_all`, `BC_hist`, `BC_hist, \Delta \delta SM = 0`, and residual difference) indicates soil-moisture-change terms materially alter inferred `LH` anomalies and seasonal curves.
- **Fig. 6**: Ancillary fields (`SM_{30d}`, `P_{30d}`, `-E_{30d}`, `-R_{30d}`) show coherent sign structures tied to AI percentile and month, supporting hydrologic control of flux partition shifts.
- **Figs. 8-14** ("month sorted by increasing AI"): Main diagnostics repeated in AI-ordered month space show stronger monotonicity in some bands and clearer low-AI/high-AI contrasts.
- **Figs. 16-19** (supplementary regional breakdown): tropical and midlatitude regions retain mixed but interpretable patterns; high-latitude regional panels are often sparse/hatched, indicating limited valid samples.
