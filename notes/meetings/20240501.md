- Spatial maps imply a contrast between smoother large-scale `\Delta\delta T` structure and much patchier `\Delta\delta a(...)_{850}` structure, interpreted as a more heterogeneous dynamical contribution.
- The deck tests how much of the mapped dynamical-looking structure survives spatial smoothing (`10 grid sm`, `30 grid sm`), implying skepticism about unsmoothed small-scale anomalies.
- Late slides split to “Cold”, “Summer Hot”, and “Summer Cold” composites, suggesting regime dependence in both thermodynamic (`\Delta\delta T`) and advection-related fields.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- The `20240501 meeting_notes.pdf` appears to be slide snapshots (no readable speaker-note prose), so interpretation is figure-inference only.
- Hatching covers many central bins in the slope heatmaps; mid-percentile/month claims are lower confidence than edge bins.
- The dramatic texture in unsmoothed `\Delta\delta a(...)_{850}` maps is not robust to coarse smoothing, so fine-scale sign changes are likely noise/artifact-sensitive.
- Several slides are near-duplicates with only labels (`Doy clima`, `Cold`, smoothing amount) changed; narrative confidence should come from cross-slide consistency, not one panel.
- Color limits are symmetric and fixed, which helps comparability, but weak summer features may be visually over-interpreted without uncertainty overlays.
