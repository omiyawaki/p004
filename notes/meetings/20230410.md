1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck centers on tropical land annual-mean (`MMM, Land, Tropics, ANN`) structure in hot-extreme change, using percentile-conditioned diagnostics of `T2m`.
- Across MMM and most individual models, warming of hot tails is stronger than cooler quantiles, with the strongest amplification near upper `T2m` percentiles.
- Several figures argue that hot-extreme changes covary with hydroclimate state: strongest `ΔδT95` occurs in drier/extreme-dry regimes, while wetter regimes cluster at smaller `ΔδT95`.
- Energy-partition diagnostics support a land-surface coupling story: `ΔδT95` tends to rise with sensible-heat anomalies and fall with latent-heat anomalies.
- The talk appears to move from distributional temperature diagnostics to mechanistic links with precipitation/flux variables, then to model-by-model robustness checks.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slides 2-7: Contour panels (MMM + per-model) show percentile-dependent warming asymmetry over tropical land; several models (e.g., CanESM5, MIROC6, TaiESM1) show strong upper-tail enhancement.
- Slide 6: Side-by-side MMM contours indicate a stronger corner/tail feature in one formulation, suggesting metric choice materially changes inferred tail amplification.
- Slide 8: Monthly global maps of `ΔδT2m95` show broad high-latitude cool anomalies with tropical/subtropical warm anomalies; right-column companion maps suggest state-dependent seasonal context rather than uniform global behavior.
- Slides 9-12: Tropical-land ANN scatter densities show (i) larger `ΔδT95` under negative `δP95`, (ii) broader/higher `ΔδT95` spread at low mean precipitation, (iii) positive relation with `ΔδSH95`, and (iv) negative relation with `ΔδLH95`.
- Slides 13-15: Model-by-model (`MI`) scatters reproduce the same qualitative coupling patterns for `δp95`, mean precipitation, and `p95`, supporting cross-model consistency with notable spread.
