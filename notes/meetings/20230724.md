1) Key Narrative Claims
- Core claim: LH response patterns are more recoverable from BC transforms when operation order preserves more SM-distribution information; `BC(mean/hot SM)` generally outperforms `mean/hot BC(SM)`.
- For SSP370-historical, skill is high for mean-day LH (R=0.93/0.98), moderate-high for hot-day LH (R=0.80/0.89), and lower but still substantial for hot-minus-mean LH (R=0.75/0.83).
- In the "Historical BC prediction" block, all skills degrade (mean: 0.54/0.57; hot: 0.46/0.51; hot-minus-mean: 0.28/0.38), implying limited transferability across historical-constrained prediction setup.
- Intermodel-variance section argues partial explanatory power: mapwise correlation shown as mostly positive, with reported means `Global R = 0.64`, `Tropical R = 0.72`; corresponding R2 summary reports `Global R = 0.41`, `Tropical R = 0.52`.
- Backup "dSM" section suggests anomaly-based BC can reproduce in-sample structure well (R=0.97/0.82/0.74), but does not remove historical-prediction degradation.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slides 3-8: paired global maps repeatedly show close spatial resemblance for mean/hot/hot-minus-mean LH between actual and BC reconstructions, with best match for mean-day and weakest for hot-minus-mean.
- Slide 9 (CESM2 Indochina): LH-SM scatter is nonlinear with regime shift/breakpoint behavior; fitted lines and mean markers indicate method sensitivity to sampling/conditioning choices.
- Slides 11-16: historical-BC versions preserve broad large-scale patterns but mute/shift regional anomalies, consistent with lower R values.
- Slides 18-19: intermodel maps show broad positive correspondence but with strong spatial heterogeneity in explained variance (high in some tropical/subtropical zones, low patches elsewhere).
- Slides 20 and 22: multi-model line plots (SM and dSM variants) show large intermodel spread and occasional sharp LH collapse at high SM or positive dSM, while MMM remains smoother.
- Slide 24 (CESM2 Central Plains): local scatter again shows strong nonlinearity and differing slopes by fit choice, reinforcing process/regime dependence.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Frame main results around operation-order sensitivity and statistic hierarchy: mean-day easiest, hot-day intermediate, hot-minus-mean hardest.
- Separate "in-sample reconstruction skill" from "historical-transfer skill" as distinct claims; current figures support the former more strongly than the latter.
- Add a compact methods box clarifying BC definitions (`BC(SM)`, `BC(mean SM)`, `BC(hot day SM)`, and dSM variants) to avoid ambiguity across repeated panels.
- Use intermodel section as qualified evidence (partial explanatory power), with explicit caveats on spatial heterogeneity and model spread.
- Keep regional scatter examples (Indochina, Central Plains) as mechanistic anchors showing nonlinear LH-SM structure that global maps alone obscure.
