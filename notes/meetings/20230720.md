1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck argues that latent heat (LH) change patterns are recoverable from soil-moisture-based BC methods, with highest skill for mean-day response and lower skill for hot-day and hot-minus-mean response.
- In the main MMM SSP370-historical diagnostics, operation order matters little for mean-day LH (`mean(BC(SM))` vs `BC(mean(SM))`, R=0.93-0.94), and only modestly for hot-day/hot-minus-mean (R=0.75-0.83).
- Portability to historical-based prediction is much weaker: the same diagnostics drop to R=0.54-0.57 (mean-day), 0.46-0.51 (hot-day), and 0.28-0.38 (hot-minus-mean).
- Intermodel-variance framing claims moderate explanatory power for hot-minus-mean LH structure (global mean R=0.58, tropical mean R=0.61), not near-complete closure.
- Backup slides suggest using soil-moisture anomaly (`dSM`) can recover SSP370-historical fields at similar skill to the main setup (R=0.97/0.82/0.74).

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Organize results around a clear skill hierarchy by target metric: mean-day > hot-day > hot-minus-mean.
- Separate in-regime reconstruction claims (higher R in SSP370-historical diagnostics) from transferability claims (lower R in historical-BC prediction).
- Keep intermodel-variance claims conservative and explicitly tie them to moderate correlation levels and model-structure spread seen in the LH-SM curves.
