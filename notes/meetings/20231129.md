1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck centers on a latitude-dependent seasonality of percentile warming (`ΔδT`): tropical hot-tail amplification is strongest in local summer, while extratropical structure is strongest in winter and favors larger relative warming of colder percentiles.
- A surface-flux-repartition framing is presented as a plausible first-order explanation in the tropics, with `SM`-linked `LH` suppression on hot/summer conditions as the main mechanism candidate.
- For extratropics, the slides argue surface energy-balance terms alone are insufficient for the full pattern; freezing/melt effects appear as a specific feature, while atmospheric dynamics remain unresolved and explicitly flagged for follow-up.
- Budyko-curve decomposition is used to claim that amplified `SM` anomalies substantially shape `ΔδLH` structure, especially in tropical land regions.
- The back half shifts toward paper framing: convert the results into a question-driven outline and add regional-variation motivation (especially tropical summertime hot-day anomalies).

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Fig. 1 (annual + seasonal percentile structure): annual `ΔδT` contrasts between tropics and extratropics are clear; monthly panels show tropical positive warm-tail anomalies in summer and strong extratropical winter structure.
- Fig. 2 (surface energy-balance components by percentile/month): tropical panels show coherent `Δ∂SH` and `Δ∂LH` changes consistent with repartitioning; extratropical panels are noisier/hatched and do not cleanly reproduce the `ΔδT` seasonal pattern.
- Fig. 2 with freezing-line overlay (extratropics): an added 0C contour highlights where `Δ∂G` anomalies align with winter suppression features, supporting a melt/phase-change contribution but not a full closure.
- Fig. 3 (Budyko/LH decomposition, tropics): the decomposition panels indicate a strong role for the `ΔδSM` term in producing summer hot-percentile `ΔδLH` anomalies; conceptual panels map this to water-limited vs energy-limited behavior.
- SM panel (tropics): soil-moisture anomalies are drier for hot/summer bins and wetter for colder/winter bins, consistent with the LH-SM storyline.
- Global maps (`ΔδT`, `ΔδLH`, `ΔδLH_BC`, `ΔδLH_BChist`, pathway maps): broad tropical/extratropical sign structures are visually consistent across observed and Budyko-reconstructed LH diagnostics, with decomposition maps used to attribute regional patterning.
- Addendum anomaly-definition slides (`seasonal median` vs `annual+percentile mean`): sign/season patterns persist across formulations, suggesting core qualitative conclusions are not purely a normalization artifact.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- The `meeting_notes.pdf` is partly interpretive narration rather than raw speaker notes (e.g., "As you mentioned", "incorporating user interpretation"), so it should be treated as secondary commentary, not primary evidence.
- Several notes pages are repetitive or overconfident relative to what is directly plotted; use deck figures as the source of truth when conflicts arise.
- Sign semantics for `LH` and related flux terms are not consistently explained on every slide; physical interpretation (cooling vs warming contribution) needs explicit convention checks in plotting scripts.
- Many panels contain hatched/masked regions and weak color amplitudes; inferred mechanism in those bins is low confidence.
- The extratropical "dynamics" explanation is a placeholder in this deck: it is asserted as needed but not diagnosed with explicit advection/atmospheric-budget terms yet.
- Regional statements (e.g., localized longitudinal anomaly features) appear in notes text but are not always cleanly demonstrated in the visible plotted panels here.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Keep the manuscript structure question-driven (seasonal `ΔδT` pattern -> mechanism tests -> pathway attribution -> regional contrasts), matching the slide outline.
- Present a two-track mechanism narrative: tropical `SM`-`LH` pathway appears comparatively well supported; extratropical winter behavior likely requires melt/phase-change plus explicit atmospheric dynamics diagnostics.
- Add an explicit atmospheric-layer energy-budget section (advection, radiative tendencies, turbulent exchange) before making strong extratropical causal claims.
- Include robustness checks on anomaly definitions and percentile framing, since addendum slides show similar qualitative structure under alternate baselines.
- Keep attribution language calibrated: "consistent with" for tropical pathway evidence; "hypothesized/needs testing" for extratropical dynamics.
- Use regional tropical summertime anomalies as a dedicated subsection, but gate claims by significance masks and sampling density.
