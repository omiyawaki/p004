# 2022-12-07 meeting

## 1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck frames a research plan around amplified hot-day warming (`T^{>95} - mean T`) and asks whether its multimodel spread is physically consistent with hot-day `SH` and `LH` changes.
- Background claim: regions with stronger hot-day amplification tend to co-locate with larger hot-day sensible-heat increases, interpreted as reduced evaporative cooling efficiency.
- A central hypothesis is that present-day hydroclimate regime representation (Budyko-curve distribution of days) may explain intermodel spread in future hot-day `LH` response.
- The final stated goal is emergent-constraint style: use observations to narrow uncertainty in projected amplified hot-day warming.

## 2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Intro build slides progressively add the full question set; many pages are animation/build duplicates rather than new analysis.
- Global MMM SSP370 maps show amplified hot-day warming hotspots over parts of subtropical/tropical land (notably South/Southeast Asia and northern South America) and weaker/negative signals over some high-latitude oceans.
- Companion `SH` and `LH` maps show expected partition contrast: where hot-day `SH` anomalies are positive, `LH` anomalies are often negative.
- STDEV panels indicate largest intermodel spread in selected tropical land regions and some high-latitude zones.
- Cross-model scatter for the tropical box (`JJA [+13.7,+106.2]`) shows strong anti-correlation between `ΔSH^{>95}` and `ΔLH^{>95}` (`R ≈ -0.89`).
- Additional scatters suggest hot-day amplification increases with `ΔSH^{>95}` (`R^2 ≈ 0.55`) and decreases with `ΔLH^{>95}` (`R^2 ≈ 0.71`).
- Model-by-model Budyko-style `LH/RSFC` vs `SM` panels are used as process context; emergent-constraint links from historical `EF` to future `ΔLH` appear weak-to-moderate in this draft (`R ≈ +0.05` for one metric, `R ≈ +0.34` for another).
- Final maps include amplification ratio and its intermodel spread, with arrows highlighting recurring hotspot regions.

## 4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Use this deck as a motivation/results-bridge section: phenomenon (`ΔT^{>95}` amplification) -> energetic partition (`ΔSH`, `ΔLH`) -> hydroclimate-regime explanation attempt.
- Keep one focused regional testbed (the tropical `JJA [+13.7,+106.2]` box) as a worked example, then expand to global maps for generality.
- Frame emergent-constraint language cautiously unless stronger out-of-sample or observational-validation evidence is added.
- In manuscript flow, separate robust findings (sign structure, strong `ΔSH`-`ΔLH` anti-correlation) from tentative findings (historical `EF` predictive skill for future `ΔLH`).
