1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck argues for a moisture-state control on latent heat (`LH`) behavior across three regions: Southeast Asia, Zambia, and Amazon.
- Monthly `LH`-vs-`SM` curves are treated as piecewise regimes (energy-limited vs moisture-limited), with percentile anchors (`95`, `50`) used to track where hot-tail conditions sit on those curves.
- A simple prediction test is implied: `Predicted ΔLH(ΔSM)` versus `Actual ΔLH`, used to assess whether soil-moisture changes alone explain latent-heat changes by month.
- Cross-region synthesis slides suggest distinct hydroclimate signatures: Southeast Asia mostly high-`SM`/high-`LH`, Zambia broader dry-to-wet transition, Amazon strong curved behavior with larger seasonal excursion.
- Final diagnostics shift from `LH` to partition metrics (`EF`, `LH/(LH+SH)`), implying the broader claim is about state-dependent surface-flux partitioning, not just raw `LH`.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slide 2 (Southeast Asia): monthly scatter clouds show a hump/plateau `LH(SM)` structure in many months; red dashed thresholds appear only on this raw-scatter panel.
- Slide 3 (Southeast Asia): black piecewise lines approximate slide-2 clouds well in monsoon months (May-October) but fit is visibly weaker in shoulder/dry months.
- Slide 4 (Southeast Asia skill check): `Actual ΔLH` is often less negative than predicted in early/late-year months, suggesting missing processes beyond `ΔSM`.
- Slides 5-6 (Amazon): `LH(SM)` curves show strong curvature and seasonal migration; prediction-vs-actual panels show mixed skill and systematic offsets in several months.
- Slides 7-8 (Zambia): pronounced dry-season low-`LH` branch and wet-season high-`LH` branch; prediction-vs-actual agreement is month-dependent, with clear mismatch clusters.
- Slide 9 (all regions, `LH` vs `SM` with month numbers): seasonal trajectories form loops/branches, indicating hysteresis-like behavior rather than a single-valued annual curve.
- Slide 10 (`EF` vs `SM`): monotonic increase with `SM` in all regions, with strongest spread/outliers in Amazon and one strong Southeast Asia low-`EF` outlier.
- Slide 11 (`LH/(LH+SH)` vs `SM`): smooth monotonic increase and tighter structure than slide 10, especially in Zambia, supporting a robust partitioning relationship.
