## 1) Key narrative claims
- Amplified hot-day warming is defined as \(\Delta T^{95} - \Delta \overline{T}\); it is broadly positive over land and negative in parts of the high-latitude oceans (slides 2-4).
- The seasonal cycle of amplified hot-day warming is large, often comparable to annual-mean signal size (slides 8-11).
- Peak amplification generally aligns with local summertime in many land regions, with notable regional exceptions (slides 11-13).
- Surface turbulent flux repartitioning (reduced LH, compensating SH) is proposed as a core mechanism, strongly consistent in tropics, partial in midlatitudes, weak in high latitudes (slides 14-20).
- Budyko-curve-based reconstruction reproduces key LH anomaly structure, supporting a decomposition framework (slides 27-30).
- Within that decomposition, soil moisture change (\(\Delta SM\)) is presented as the dominant term for LH response; \(\Delta BC\) is secondary (slide 30).
- Two pathway framing (Pathway 1 early-summer-like vs Pathway 2 later-summer-like / water-limited behavior) is used to explain spatial hotspot differences (slides 31-40).

## 4) Implications for paper outline
- **Intro / motivation**: emphasize why annual-mean diagnostics miss key risk-relevant behavior; use seasonality-first framing from slides 8-13.
- **Results 1 (phenomenology)**: global + zonal characterization of amplified hot-day warming magnitude and timing.
- **Results 2 (mechanism diagnosis)**: flux repartitioning analysis by latitude band, explicitly separating where mechanism is strong/partial/insufficient.
- **Results 3 (process decomposition)**: Budyko-based LH decomposition with uncertainty and validation against "Actual".
- **Results 4 (regional hotspots)**: pathway attribution maps for major tropical/subtropical hotspots; clarify pathway definitions and criteria reproducibly.
- **Discussion**: treat high-latitude amplification as partially unresolved; likely needs additional mechanisms beyond SM-LH pathway framework.
- **Methods/robustness priorities**: formal significance, sensitivity to percentile definition, model spread diagnostics, and transparent criteria for pathway assignment.
