1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck argues that timing of hot-day amplification is seasonally structured by hemisphere: tropical and extratropical maxima trace a diagonal month-vs-area pattern after winter solstice, with peak timing shifting by latitude band (slides 2-5, 8-12).
- Applying a `Max > 0.5 K` filter appears to isolate a stronger warm-season signal, while all-grid composites retain the same phase structure but with weaker amplitude (slides 4-7).
- The strongest positive `ΔδT^95` seasonality is in the tropics, midlatitudes are near-neutral to weakly negative in many months, and high latitudes show large negative values outside a short warm-season window (slide 12).
- The deck tests thermodynamic decomposition by contrasting Temperature with `SH` and `LH` structures; visually, `LH` patterns depart more strongly from the temperature baseline than `SH`, especially in extratropics (slides 13-18).
- July and January maps suggest month-dependent regime classes over land, consistent with a claim that the dominant mechanism/timing state changes seasonally and regionally rather than being globally uniform (slides 19-20).

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slide 2: Tropical gridpoint and cumulative-area heatmaps both show a coherent descending ridge (roughly late boreal spring to autumn), indicating phase-locked seasonal timing in hot-day behavior.
- Slide 3: Extratropics show a broader, noisier ridge embedded in large blue regions, implying stronger heterogeneity and weaker all-area coherence than tropics.
- Slides 4-5: Side-by-side `Max > 0.5 K` versus all-grid views preserve pattern shape but sharpen contrast under thresholding, especially in extratropical bands.
- Slides 6-7: Area-aggregated monthly curves quantify the contrast: positive tropical amplitudes and substantially negative extratropical values for much of the year, with brief sign reversal near peak season.
- Slides 8-9: Binning into 2.5% intervals smooths noise but keeps the diagonal timing ridge; extratropical structure still shows strong low- and high-area blue zones.
- Slide 10: Global map of months-since-winter-solstice timing shows substantial regional spread across land, with many subtropical/midlatitude regions peaking in boreal warm season and other regions offset.
- Slides 11-12: Tropical/midlatitude/high-latitude split confirms a gradient from coherent tropical positive timing to near-neutral midlatitudes and strongly negative high-latitude off-season values.
- Slides 13-14 (Tropics): Temperature reference is broadly positive; `SH` remains muted/mixed while `LH` is predominantly negative-toned, indicating strong latent-related contrast in tropical composites.
- Slides 15-18 (Extratropics): Temperature reference includes large blue sectors; `SH` and `LH` panels show distinct subpatterns, with `LH` presenting a concentrated warm-season stripe against cooler background states.
- Slides 19-20: July and January categorical world maps show mostly one dominant class (green) with regional patches of alternatives, and hemisphere-dependent seasonal swaps in patch location.
