1) Key Narrative Claims
- Main claim: BC using full SM information can reconstruct spatial patterns of LH response, but skill depends strongly on operation order and target statistic (mean, hot-day, hot-minus-mean).
- For SSP370-historical (shown for CESM2 and MMM), mean-day LH is highly recoverable (R ~0.97-0.99), hot-day is moderate-to-strong (R ~0.76-0.83), and hot-minus-mean is lower but positive (R ~0.67-0.74).
- A “hot-day-only BC” pathway fails for hot-day LH (R = -0.11), implying information in non-hot-day structure or sequencing is essential.
- Historical-BC prediction is much weaker (R ~0.28-0.57), especially for hot-minus-mean, suggesting portability across baseline climates is limited.
- Intermodel-variance section argues moderate explained variance for hot-minus-mean patterns (global R ~0.44-0.54; tropical R ~0.58-0.64).
