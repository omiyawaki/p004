1) Key Narrative Claims
- The central claim is that the seasonal cycle of percentile warming (`Δ∂T`) differs by region: summertime amplification in the tropics versus wintertime amplification in the extratropics (p12).
- The proposed mechanistic frame is surface-energy-balance consistency, with tropical behavior tied to soil-moisture-mediated latent-heat changes (LH-SM relation), while extratropical behavior may involve snowmelt and dynamics that are not yet resolved (p12).
- Fig. 1 sequence argues that absolute warming alone does not explain percentile-season structure; anomaly framing (seasonal median and annual+percentile mean references) reveals stronger structure, especially in extratropics (pp14-17).
- Fig. 2/3 sequence implies decomposition of `Δ∂T` into radiative, sensible, latent, and ground components and then focuses on latent-heat pathway interpretation using water-limited vs energy-limited regimes (pp4, 18, 20, 23).
- A stated forward claim is regionally heterogeneous tropical summertime hot-day warming, motivating a regionalized analysis beyond zonal/tropical means (p12; supported by tropical maps on pp8-9, 11).

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Framing: lead with the seasonal asymmetry question (`tropical-summer vs extratropical-winter amplification`) as the organizing thesis.
- Results Section 1: document `Δ∂T` seasonal-percentile structure using Fig. 1 style diagnostics, including anomaly definitions and why absolute warming is insufficient.
- Results Section 2: mechanistic decomposition (`Δ∂R`, `Δ∂SH`, `Δ∂LH`, `Δ∂G`) with explicit significance masking language and a clear statement of what is robust versus tentative.
- Results Section 3: tropical LH-SM pathway analysis using conceptual regime framing plus sensitivity tests (bias correction, fixed-`ΔδSM` experiments).
- Results Section 4: extratropical mechanisms should be split into "supported now" (seasonal structure, possible snowmelt linkage) and "open" (dynamics quantification), avoiding over-claiming.
- Results Section 5: regional tropical heterogeneity (map-based) should be elevated from appendix-level to a core subsection since it conditions mechanism generality.
- Discussion: include an explicit uncertainty paragraph on figure-version evolution, hatched-cell prevalence, and diagnostic limitations in dynamic terms.
