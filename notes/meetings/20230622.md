1) Key Narrative Claims
- The main claim is that a BC-derived latent heat (LH) response reproduces the spatial pattern of modeled LH change well for mean conditions, and reasonably for hot/extreme conditions.
- For MMM SSP370-HISTORICAL (JJA+DJF), agreement is strongest for mean-day LH (R=0.98), weaker for hot-day LH (R=0.76), and weakest for hot-minus-mean LH anomalies (R=0.69).
- A mechanism slide (Indochina monthly LH-SM curves) suggests a piecewise soil-moisture control with regime thresholds; this is used as the basis for the BC reconstruction logic.
- A sensitivity/check appears to hold soil moisture fixed to historical (`...BChist`), which substantially damps reconstructed amplitudes for hot and hot-minus-mean responses despite retaining some broad pattern similarity.
- In extras, CESM2 reproduces the same hierarchy (R=0.99, 0.83, 0.67), supporting that skill degradation from mean to extremes is not MMM-specific.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slide 2: Indochina monthly LH vs SM panels show seasonal shifts in inferred critical soil moisture (`SM_c`) and distinct wet-/dry-side behavior; precipitation-colored markers imply hydroclimate-state dependence.
- Slide 3: Global maps of mean-day LH response (actual vs BC-derived) are visually very close in sign and structure; strongest positive changes over many northern land regions and mixed drying-linked negatives in subtropics.
- Slide 4: Hot-day LH maps show larger regional mismatches (notably stronger negative patches in the actual field over parts of the Americas/Eurasia) while still capturing broad continental-scale contrasts.
- Slide 5: Hot-minus-mean LH maps are predominantly negative over many land areas in both fields, but BC underestimates magnitude/texture; this is the hardest target.
- Slide 6: Model-level correlation map (`MI SSP370-HISTORICAL`) indicates mostly positive local correspondence for hot-minus-mean anomalies, with localized weak/negative pockets.
- Slides 7-9: `BChist` tests (historical-SM-constrained BC) show strong attenuation of spatial amplitude versus actual changes across mean/hot/hot-minus-mean panels.
- Slides 11-13 (extras): CESM2 repeats the same three-panel diagnostics with high mean-day skill and moderate extreme-day skill.
- Slide 14 (extras): 16-model Indochina June warming stamps show inter-model spread in sign/magnitude and spatial pattern complexity.
- Slide 15 (extras): 16-model LH-SM phase-space panels show broad structural consistency (rising then flattening/declining LH) but clear model spread in `SM_c` and curve shape.
- Slides 16-17 (extras): Four Corners percentile-warming curves for CanESM5 vs MPI-ESM1-2-LR differ strongly in level and tail behavior; overplotting many ensemble members suggests robust model-dependent shape differences.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Position BC as a "pattern-capture" framework with explicitly tiered confidence: strongest for mean-state LH change, moderate for hot-day change, weaker for hot-minus-mean diagnostics.
- Add a dedicated "mechanism and limits" subsection centered on soil-moisture-regime dependence (`SM_c`) and why fixed-historical-SM reconstructions lose amplitude under warming.
- For results structure, pair global MMM evidence with one single-model deep dive (CESM2) plus one regional process case (Indochina) to show scale consistency and model spread.
- Include a targeted inter-model heterogeneity section (e.g., Four Corners percentile curves) to motivate uncertainty framing rather than presenting a single emergent-shape story.
- In methods/appendix, document provenance caveats (re-exported slides, sparse notes text) and avoid overstating unsupported narrative details.
