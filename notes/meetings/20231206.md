1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck argues that the seasonal cycle of percentile warming (`ΔδT`) differs by latitude band: tropical amplification is concentrated in local warm-season hot percentiles, while extratropical structure is strongest in cold-season months.
- A surface-energy partition story is proposed as the main tropical mechanism, with `SM` control on `LH` used to explain why hot/summer bins warm differently from cooler bins.
- The extratropical mechanism is presented as unresolved: some signal is consistent with melt/ground-flux effects, but the slide explicitly says dynamics still need investigation.
- The meeting reframes the study around manuscript questions (seasonal `ΔδT`, mechanism tests, tropical regional variability) rather than only showing diagnostics.
- Addendum slides suggest the qualitative seasonal-percentile pattern is robust to anomaly-definition choices (seasonal-median vs annual/percentile-mean references).

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Fig. 1 (tropics vs extratropics, percentile x month): tropics show modest warm-tail enhancement in warm months; extratropics show a stronger seasonal sign reversal with large-amplitude winter structure.
- Fig. 2 (decomposition into `Δ∂R`, `Δ∂SH`, `Δ∂LH`, `Δ∂G`): tropical `SH`/`LH` terms show coherent seasonal-percentile structure, while extratropical panels are less clean and include many hatched/low-confidence bins.
- Fig. 3 (Budyko/LH-SM framework): conceptual water-limited to energy-limited transition and decomposition panels are used to attribute tropical `LH` changes partly to `SM` anomalies.
- Global map sequence (slides 7-10): `ΔδT` is broadly positive over many tropical land areas in JJA+DJF; `ΔδLH` is often negative in tropical land regions; Budyko-based reconstructions (`ΔδLH_BC`, `ΔδLH_BChist`) reproduce broad spatial patterns with residual differences.
- Updated/repeated figure set (slides 13-19): the same headline patterns are revisited with clearer emphasis boxes in Fig. 2 and a shortened Fig. 3 decomposition row.
- Fig. 1 addendum (slide 23): latitude split (tropics, midlatitudes, high latitudes) shows strongest seasonal reversal at high latitudes and weakest structure in the tropics.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- The title on page 1 reads `20241206 meeting` even though the file is for 2023-12-06, so the deck likely contains a date-label typo.
- `meeting_notes.pdf` appears to be slide snapshots on letter pages (not rich speaker notes); `pdftotext` is empty and OCR is noisy, so the deck visuals are the primary source of truth.
- Several extracted symbols are OCR-corrupted (`Δ∂`/subscripts/signs), so any exact algebraic form from OCR alone is low confidence.
- Many bins in percentile-month heatmaps are hatched/masked, indicating limited robustness/significance; mechanistic claims in those regions should be treated as tentative.
- Fig. 2 includes manual emphasis marks (solid/dashed red boxes, black curve overlay), which look like exploratory annotations rather than finalized diagnostic tests.
- Global map slides are informative but label-dense and partly cropped in rendered pages; fine regional claims (small-scale hotspots) should be verified from source plotting scripts before publication use.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Keep a question-driven structure: (i) seasonal-latitudinal `ΔδT` phenomenology, (ii) mechanism tests via surface-energy decomposition, (iii) tropical regional pattern analysis.
- Split attribution confidence by region: stronger "consistent with SM-LH control" language for tropics; explicitly provisional language for extratropics pending dynamic diagnostics.
- Include an uncertainty/robustness subsection covering anomaly-definition sensitivity and masked-bin treatment, since both are central to this deck’s interpretation.
- Use the map sequence as a regional-evidence bridge between zonal/percentile diagnostics and geographically explicit impacts, but avoid overclaiming where reconstruction residuals are visible.
- In the manuscript narrative, treat Fig. 2 emphasis boxes as hypothesis-generating focal regions that need follow-up quantitative tests, not final causal proof.
