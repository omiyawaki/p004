1) Key Narrative Claims
- The central hypothesis is that winter percentile-dependent near-surface warming over NH high latitudes is dynamically linked to lower-tropospheric temperature advection, with strongest structure in late fall-to-winter months.
- Comparing `tas` vs `-cp(udxT+vdyT)_850`, the broad seasonal pattern (suppressed hot-day warming, amplified cold-day warming) appears qualitatively aligned, but map-level spatial agreement is weak for key regions.
- Recasting to `ta850` improves consistency with 850-hPa advection for hot extremes (>95th percentile), suggesting the advection diagnostic is more directly tied to free-tropospheric temperature than to 2 m temperature.
- For cold extremes (<5th percentile), the mismatch remains substantial even with `ta850`, indicating additional processes beyond 850-hPa horizontal advection are needed.
- A conceptual framing is proposed: relatively bottom-up influence on hot days vs top-down dynamical influence on cold days (supported qualitatively by the historical 850-hPa omega panel and percentile structure).

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Seasonal-percentile heatmaps (`T`/`T850` vs advection) show a robust dipole-like structure across percentiles: upper-percentile cooling/suppressed warming and lower-percentile amplified warming in winter months, with advection panels showing comparable timing.
- Global maps for early winter and winter (>95th) show strong NH high-latitude signals in `deltaT`, while advection anomalies are more zonally/regionally patchy; qualitative overlap exists but with sign/extent disagreements in midlatitudes.
- Annotated NH-strip panels explicitly highlight that temperature response bands and advection/ground-flux bands do not line up cleanly in latitude, motivating skepticism about one-to-one attribution.
- The "HL seasonal cycle" synthesis panel places 2 m warming, 850-hPa advection, and ground flux side-by-side and adds historical 850-hPa omega, visually supporting asymmetry between hot and cold tails.
- Surface-energy-budget decomposition (>95 and <5) indicates `R_sfc` and especially `SW` often carry stronger coherent regional signals than `LW`, `SH`, and `LH`, with strongest anomalies over land hot spots and weaker/more mixed signs elsewhere.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- Notes themselves repeatedly state mismatches between amplified tail warming patterns and advection response; this is a major internal caveat, not a minor residual.
- Several slides in the `<5` block are still titled "Winter, >95 percentile," and season labels mix `NDJ+MJJ` with `DJF+JJA` across paired maps, suggesting copy/paste or labeling inconsistency that could mislead interpretation.
- Many percentile-month cells are hatched/masked; effective sample/significance coverage is uneven, so apparent structures in unhatched cells may overstate robustness if not area-weighted or uncertainty-quantified.
- The advection unit scaling changes across panels (e.g., W kg^-1 vs pressure-weighted W m^-2), which is physically valid but easy to conflate when making visual causal claims.
- Deck and notes PDF metadata show creation on 2026-02-28 for a "20240301" meeting package, implying post-hoc regeneration/version drift risk relative to original analysis state.
- The notes PDF has 35 pages while the deck has 32, with several sparse/blank or repeated pages; this increases risk of overcounting repeated evidence as independent support.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Position advection as a partial explanatory mechanism: strong for timing/structure (especially for `T850` hot-tail behavior), insufficient alone for full `tas` and cold-tail spatial patterns.
- Build the results narrative in three layers: (i) seasonal-percentile diagnostics, (ii) spatial pattern tests, (iii) residual-process diagnosis (surface energy budget + vertical motion/PBL mediation).
- Treat cold-tail amplification as the key unresolved target for mechanism development; explicitly test candidates beyond horizontal advection (cloud-radiative effects, stability/PBL coupling, circulation regime shifts).
- Separate claims for free-tropospheric vs near-surface temperature responses to avoid overextending the 850-hPa advection argument to `tas`.
- Add a methods/robustness subsection that standardizes season definitions, harmonizes panel labels, and reports uncertainty/significance coverage to preempt artifact-driven criticism.
