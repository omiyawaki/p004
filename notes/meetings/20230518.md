1) Key Narrative Claims
- Indochina is used as a case study of hot-tail warming seasonality (`ΔδT` and `ΔδT^95`) under `SSP370-HISTORICAL`, with strongest positive anomalies in boreal summer and weaker/negative anomalies in late spring and late year.
- The deck argues hot-day latent heat (`ΔLH`, red) diverges from all-day latent heat (black), especially in early monsoon, and frames this as a moisture-limitation signal on hot days versus broader energy-limitation behavior in seasonal means.
- A nonlinear `LH(SM)` relation with an inferred threshold (`SM_c` around 35 kg m^-2 in MMM) is presented as the organizing mechanism separating moisture-limited and energy-limited regimes.
- Event-relative composites (days around `T > 95` events) show co-evolution of `LH`, `SM`, and `P`, with strongest anomalies in May-September and much weaker anomalies in cool-season months.
- Multi-model panels suggest June hot-tail warming over Indochina is robustly positive in many models, but amplitude and local sign vary substantially by model.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slides 2-3: Monthly Indochina maps and mask show `ΔδT^95` peaking in June-August (large positive core) with a clear seasonal reversal toward neutral/negative in November-December.
- Slides 4-6: Annual-cycle diagnostics show a sharp hot-day `ΔLH` minimum near early June (about -16 to -17 W m^-2), much deeper than all-day `ΔLH`, while `ΔδT` peaks near early summer.
- Slides 7-8: `LH` rises with `SM` then flattens past `SM_c`; hot-day `SM` seasonal cycle stays below all-day values most of year and approaches/surpasses the threshold later in the wet season.
- Slides 9-11: Around hot events, June-September show pronounced negative `LH` anomalies near day 0 and concurrent `SM`/`P` anomalies, whereas many other months remain close to zero.
- Slides 13-14: Historical vs future precipitation annual cycles are similar in shape, with future shifts modest relative to the hot-day vs all-day separation.
- Slides 15-16: Model spread is large; estimated `SM_c` spans roughly low-20s to low-40s kg m^-2 across models, indicating regime-threshold uncertainty.
- Slides 17-18 (`fourcorners`): percentile-shape comparisons between CanESM5 and MPI-ESM1-2-LR are shown, with strong offset between models across the full `T` percentile range.
