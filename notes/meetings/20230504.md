1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck tests a simple moisture-control hypothesis for hot-tail latent-heat change (`ΔδLH`) using monthly `LH`-`SM` relationships, with Southeast Asia as the first detailed case.
- A piecewise-linear fit to `LH(SM)` is used to predict percentile shifts (`Predicted ΔLH(ΔSM)`), then compared against actual shifts; this is presented as a mechanistic benchmark rather than a pure statistical fit.
- Southeast Asia results are mixed by season: transitional/wet-season months sometimes align in sign/magnitude, but many months show clear offsets between predicted and actual high-percentile changes.
- The analysis is then generalized to Amazon and a dry-border region (`~ CA/AZ/MEX border`) to show regime dependence (humid basin vs arid transition zone).
- A global map (`R(ΔδLH^95, ΔδSM^95)`) is used to motivate where soil-moisture control is likely strongest, with broad positive land correlations and weaker/negative ocean or high-latitude patches.
