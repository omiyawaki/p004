1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck argues for strong seasonality in projected changes to hot-tail temperature metrics (`ΔδT^95`) under `SSP370-HISTORICAL`, with largest positive anomalies over land and high latitudes in boreal summer.
- Percentile separation is emphasized: `99th > 95th > mean` for many seasonal-temperature diagnostics, implying disproportionate tail behavior relative to central tendency.
- A moisture-conditioned framing appears central (`q=0` and `q=q_s` panels): warming patterns and amplitudes differ depending on humidity state, especially over subtropical/tropical land.
- Hydroclimate is introduced as a coupled ingredient: monthly `P` structure and maps of mean precipitation are used to contextualize temperature-tail changes.
- The late slides propose that statistical coupling between hot-tail temperature and precipitation (`R^2(ΔδT^95, P̄)`) is weak globally but seasonally modulated, and substantially stronger when restricted to land and especially tropical land.
