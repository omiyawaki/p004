1) Key Narrative Claims
- The deck advances a state-dependent `LH-SM` framework for Indochina: low-`SM` moisture-limited increase, then high-`SM` energy-limited plateau/decline, with a representative breakpoint near `SM_c ~ 35 kg m^-2` in the MMM view.
- Hot days (red) are consistently drier than all days (black) through most months, placing heat extremes on different parts of the `LH(SM)` curve and implying distinct evaporative behavior during extremes.
- The classification schema is expanded from prior Hsu-and-Dirmeyer/Qin curve types to additional shapes, suggesting the author is trying to capture broader monthly/model structural diversity.
- Historical-to-future comparison implies monsoon-season precipitation timing/intensity shifts and a reduction in hot-day precipitation relative to all-day precipitation in both climates.
- Inter-model spread is central to the message: spatial hot-day anomaly maps and per-model `LH(SM)` panels show common qualitative structure but large model-specific differences.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slide 2: MMM scatter/fit marks `SM_c=35`; `LH` rises from roughly `~55` to `~100 W m^-2` then flattens and slightly declines at the wettest tail.
- Slide 3: Seasonal `SM` cycle shows hot days below all days year-round, with largest separation in boreal spring (`~Mar-May`) and partial convergence near late-year.
- Slides 4-7: Taxonomy progression (`111,110,011,010,001` + Qin `1n10,0n10` + new red additions `1111,0111,0101,1101`) reframes curve shapes as discrete regime classes.
- Slide 8: Monthly MMM `LH(SM)` fits show seasonal shape changes; several months (roughly `Jul-Oct`) exhibit post-peak decline at high `SM`, while early-year months are more plateau-like.
- Slide 10: `LH` anomaly composites around `T>95` days show strong negative dips in `Apr-Jun` near event day and smaller positive spikes in `Jul-Sep`, indicating month-dependent event coupling.
- Slides 13-14: In both historical and future, hot-day precipitation stays far below all-day precipitation during monsoon season; future retains this gap with modest seasonal reshaping.
- Slides 15-16: June SSP370-minus-historical hot-day `ΔδT` maps are mostly positive over Indochina in many models but include notable opposing-sign structures in some models.
- Slides 17-18: "fourcorners" percentile-warming curves differ sharply by model family (e.g., CanESM5 vs MPI-ESM1-2-LR), and ensemble members preserve that separation.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- `pdftotext` output from both slide and notes PDFs is extremely sparse (mostly titles/labels), so narrative extraction is effectively figure-only.
- The `meeting_notes.pdf` appears to be slide thumbnails, not readable presenter notes; there is no independent note text to confirm speaker intent.
- Labels `1n10` and `0n10` are non-binary and likely placeholder/notation carryover; treat these as provisional until defined in methods/code.
- "Suqin:" appears as an inline citation callout with no bibliographic detail on-slide, and may be a carryover shorthand rather than a resolved reference.
- "fourcorners" appears as an undeclared diagnostic name; without method context it is vulnerable to template carryover interpretation.
- Several slides juxtapose different diagnostics/scales without explicit normalization notes, so cross-panel magnitude comparisons should be treated cautiously.
