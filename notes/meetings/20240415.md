1) Key Narrative Claims
- The meeting’s core claim is that near-surface temperature percentile seasonality can be interpreted through lower-tropospheric horizontal temperature advection structure, using month-by-percentile heatmaps.
- A highlighted claim is that two specific month windows (approximately months 3-4 and 9-10 since winter solstice) organize strong warm/cold contrasts across percentiles, and those windows align with advection anomalies.
- The CESM2 vs RAE+TF profile comparison argues that a simple/idealized framework can reproduce much of the vertical thermal structure in the lower and middle troposphere, but with clear upper-level mismatch.
- Scatter relationships suggest that warmer states are associated with more negative advection tendency (as plotted), and perturbations (delta fields) also show a negative slope with month-dependent regression strength.
- The later slides test transformed variables (`a * advection`, `aG`) and imply that some transformed terms are weak or diffuse in percentile-month space, while gross radiative-like term `G` keeps a strong seasonal/thermal branch structure.

2) Figure-Level Highlights
- Slide 2: Baseline heatmaps show `T` with strong seasonal sign reversals across percentiles and an advection panel with smaller amplitude (about +/-0.03 W kg^-1), including many hatched (likely insignificant) cells in the center percentiles.
- Slide 3: Red boxes isolate month bands near 3-4 and 9-10, emphasizing those as candidate transition/anchor periods in both `T` and advection fields.
- Slide 4: Vertical profile (`p` vs `T`) compares CESM2 (black) and RAE+TF (red). Agreement is reasonable through much of the troposphere, but the red curve becomes unrealistically warm aloft (large divergence near top levels).
- Slide 5: Analytical expression for surface temperature (`sigma Ts^4`) is introduced, indicating a decomposition framework with shortwave, atmospheric forcing, optical depth terms, and coefficients (`tau0`, `beta`, `b`).
- Slide 6: Scatter of `T` against advection (colored by month) forms a tilted cloud; warm months cluster at high `T` and more negative plotted advection values, while cold months occupy lower `T` and broader spread.
- Slide 7: Scatter of `delta T` vs `delta(advection)` shows consistently negative monthly regression slopes but strong spread and month-to-month slope variation.
- Slide 8: Monthly line of `partial T / partial(advection)` stays negative all year, weakest magnitude near month 10 and strongest in late winter/early spring.
- Slides 9-10: `a * advection` heatmaps remain weak, with extensive hatching and strong dependence on colorbar range choice (+/-1 vs +/-0.2), indicating low robust signal except limited edge-percentile structure.
- Slide 11: `aG` heatmap shows a concentrated strong positive lobe near highest percentiles in early months and otherwise patchy/hatched structure.
- Slide 12: `T` vs `G` scatter displays a branched/nonlinear envelope with seasonal coloring; high-`G` branch is mostly warm-season points.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- The `_notes` PDF does not appear to include additional speaker-note text; interpretation must rely on figures themselves, reducing confidence in intended narrative nuance.
- Hatched regions cover substantial area in several heatmaps (especially transformed terms), so central claims should distinguish robust edge signals from non-significant interior patterns.
- Visual emphasis on month bands (red boxes) is persuasive but potentially selective; no quantitative criterion for choosing those months is shown on-slide.
- Slope interpretation in scatter plots may be sensitive to sign convention (`+u cp d_x T + v cp d_y T` vs `-(...)` used in different panels); potential confusion could invert physical interpretation if not explicitly standardized.
- Comparison of transformed advection terms across slides 9-10 is sensitive to colorbar scaling; apparent structure changes materially when range is tightened.
- The RAE+TF upper-level temperature behavior appears nonphysical or outside target validity, so framework claims should be bounded to pressure levels where agreement is demonstrated.
- Some distributions show wedge/branch geometry that could partly reflect seasonal sampling density and heteroscedasticity rather than a single mechanistic law.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Framing: Position the paper around percentile-resolved seasonal structure, then test whether advection-based and energy-balance terms explain that structure with explicit significance masks.
- Methods subsection needed: define advection sign conventions once, keep them consistent across all figures, and report how `a` and `G` are derived.
- Results sequencing: (i) baseline `T` and advection heatmaps, (ii) transition-month diagnostics, (iii) scatter-based sensitivities, (iv) transformed-term decomposition and limits.
- Robustness section should include objective month-selection tests (not only boxed visual windows), sensitivity to color scales/binning, and confidence intervals on monthly regression slopes.
- Model hierarchy narrative: present RAE+TF as a constrained explanatory model valid for selected levels/conditions, with explicit failure mode aloft.
- Discussion should separate strong conclusions (persistent negative local sensitivity, seasonal organization) from tentative ones (importance of `a * advection` interior-percentile structure).
