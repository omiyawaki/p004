1) Key Narrative Claims
- The meeting builds a snowmelt-energy narrative where high-latitude structure in `\Delta\delta G` is compared against `\Delta F_{snowmelt}` and snow-concentration-edge shifts, first in MMM and then CESM2.
- A central claim is percentile asymmetry: `97.5th` snowmelt-related flux changes are much larger and more spatially coherent than `50th` changes (shown in CESM2 and historical baselines).
- The deck pivots from snowmelt diagnostics to a dynamical/advection framing for temperature-percentile change, pairing `\Delta\delta T` with `\Delta\delta(-vc_p\partial_y\overline{T})` under multiple gradient-processing choices (`mon`, `doy`, T18).
- Method choice is treated as scientifically consequential: the same conceptual variable changes substantially across `gradT mon` vs `gradT doy`, and across `850` vs `925` hPa formulations.
- The implied storyline is that snow-edge-related surface energy signals and circulation/advection terms are jointly needed to explain regional hot-tail behavior, but with strong method-sensitivity caveats.

3) Reliability/Artifact Flags
- `20240517 meeting_notes.pdf` appears to be slide snapshots on letter pages with large blank lower area; no additional speaker-note prose was found in text extraction or visual inspection.
- OCR is useful for rough labels but error-prone for equations/subscripts (`850` vs `925`, `\delta` vs `\Delta\delta`); all key variable readings above were cross-checked visually.
- Multiple panels reuse near-identical top maps while changing only lower-panel definition; risk: over-claiming novelty if panel metadata is not tracked carefully.
- Unit and level shifts are frequent (`W m^{-2}`, `W kg^{-1}`, `K`; `850` vs `925` hPa). Direct visual comparison across these is unsafe without explicit normalization.
- Slide 16 regression map is speckled/noisy at grid scale, indicating potential sampling/noise amplification; localized extrema should be treated as low-confidence unless reproduced under smoothing/regridding.
- T18 `gradT mon` vs `gradT doy` produces large structural differences (including sign-regime shifts), which flags method dependence as a first-order uncertainty rather than a minor technical detail.
- Snow-concentration contour levels are not explicitly labeled on these pages, limiting quantitative interpretation of contour-map alignment.
- Slide 13 (`Extra`) suggests the deck may be an in-progress analysis branch rather than a finalized narrative.

4) Paper-Outline Implications
- Organize results into two linked blocks: (i) snowmelt-energy percentile diagnostics (`G`, `F_{snowmelt}`, snow-edge contours), then (ii) dynamical/advection decomposition (`\Delta\delta T` vs advection-like terms).
- Add a dedicated robustness subsection on methodological sensitivity (`gradT mon/doy`, pressure level choice, smoothing), with confidence tiers for claims that survive all variants.
- Use the `F_{snowmelt}(T)` nonlinearity figure early as mechanism context to justify why percentile-specific diagnostics are necessary.
- Treat snow-concentration overlays as qualitative support unless contour thresholds/definitions are documented; include explicit definitions in methods or supplement.
- Keep claims about local hotspots conservative; emphasize basin/continental-scale recurring patterns as primary evidence.
- Explicitly separate exploratory diagnostics (e.g., noisy grid-wise regression fields) from core evidentiary figures in the manuscript flow.
