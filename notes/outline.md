**Seasonal Cycle of Warming Across the Temperature Distribution: Part 1
– Tropical Amplification Driven by Surface Flux Repartitioning**

- **Storyline:** Introduce amplified hot day warming. Show that the
  annual mean view is misleading. Introduce the percentile-seasonal
  analysis framework. Demonstrate the contrasting seasonality (Tropics:
  summer amplification for hot days; Extratropics: winter amplification
  for cold days / winter suppression for hot days). Mechanistically
  explain the tropical pattern via surface flux repartitioning driven by
  soil moisture dynamics (using SEB and Budyko analysis). Demonstrate
  that the extratropical pattern is *not* explained by surface energy
  balance alone, except for the suppression near freezing caused by
  snow/ice melt. Conclude by highlighting the different dominant
  controls (surface moisture vs. likely atmospheric dynamics/phase
  change) in the two regions.

      mention limitations of annual mean, state goal to analyze seasonal
      cycle across the full distribution and compare
      Tropics/Extratropics.

  2.  **Methods:** Describe datasets (models, reanalysis), definition of
      differential warming (ΔδT relative to median or annual/pct mean -
      need to choose/justify), SEB analysis, Budyko decomposition
      framework.

  3.  **Results:**

        as baseline/contrast.

      - Show seasonal cycle of ΔδT vs. percentile for Tropics and
        Extratropics (e.g., Slide 11 or 32, 2023-11-29 deck),
        highlighting the contrasting patterns.

      - Show SEB analysis for Tropics (e.g., Slide 15, 2023-11-29 deck)

      - Present Budyko decomposition for Tropics (e.g., Slide 19,
        2023-11-29 deck) showing role of amplified SM decrease

  5.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the contrasting mechanisms and seasonal

**Seasonal Cycle of Warming Across the Temperature Distribution: Part 2
– Melting and Advection in High Latitudes**

- **Storyline:** This paper addresses the puzzle identified in Part 1:
  why surface energy balance cannot fully explain the unique
  high-latitude differential warming pattern, particularly the strong
  winter signal (amplified cold days, suppressed hot days). It will
  first provide definitive evidence for the role of surface phase change
  (snow/ice melt) in suppressing warming near 0°C for the hottest winter
  days. It will then present analyses of atmospheric dynamics (e.g.,
  temperature advection) to explain the amplification of warming for the
  coldest winter days and the overall structure of the differential
  warming, linking these dynamic contributions to broader Arctic
  Amplification processes.

- **Potential Outline:**

  1.  **Introduction:**

      - Briefly reference Part 1, summarizing the key finding: unique
        high-latitude differential warming patterns (especially winter
        amplification of cold, suppression of hot) are not fully
        explained by surface energy budgets alone.

      - State the specific goals of this paper: (1) Quantify the role of
        surface phase change in suppressing warming near 0°C in
        winter. (2) Investigate the role of atmospheric dynamics in
        driving the amplification of cold winter days and the overall
        differential warming pattern. (3) Frame these findings in the
        context of Arctic Amplification mechanisms.

  2.  **Methods:**

      - Describe datasets (models, reanalysis, focusing on high
        latitudes).

      - Recap definition of differential warming (ΔδT, consistent with
        Part 1).

      - Detail the surface energy balance analysis, emphasizing the
        calculation and interpretation of the melt flux term (Fmelt /
        Δ∂G).

      - Describe methods used to diagnose atmospheric dynamics (e.g.,
        calculation of temperature advection at relevant levels like 850
        hPa, atmospheric energy flux divergence, potentially the
        "Bottom-Up Top-down" framework if results are robust).

  3.  **Results:**

      - **Characterize High-Latitude Pattern:** Briefly show the
        seasonal cycle of ΔδT vs. percentile for high
        latitudes/extratropics (e.g., Slide 11 Right or 32 Right,

        detailed winter SEB analysis (e.g., Slides 7-9, 2023-11-09 deck;
        Slide 17, 2023-11-29 deck). Show the strong negative ΔFmelt /
        Δ∂G coinciding precisely with the suppressed ΔT near 0°C.
        Quantify the energy budget closure at different percentiles.

      - **Dynamics Mechanism for Winter Cold Day Amplification:**
        Present results from the atmospheric dynamics analyses (e.g.,

  4.  **Discussion:**

        suppression near 0°C, while atmospheric dynamics drive the
        amplification at the cold end.

      - Connect the findings on atmospheric dynamics explicitly to
        established Arctic Amplification mechanisms (e.g., increased
        poleward energy transport, specific feedback loops).

      - Discuss limitations (e.g., model dependency of dynamic signals,
        complexity of disentangling feedbacks, chosen level for dynamics

  5.  **Conclusion:**

      - Summarize the key findings establishing the distinct roles of
        surface phase change and atmospheric dynamics in shaping
        high-latitude differential warming, particularly in winter.

      - Reiterate the connection to Arctic Amplification.
