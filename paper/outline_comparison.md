# Comparison: initial outline vs meeting-informed outline

## Documents compared
- Initial: `paper/outline.md`
- Meeting-informed: `paper/outline_meetings.md`

## How the new outline differs

1. **Narrative center shifted**
- Initial outline centers a broad dataset stack (ERA5 + CMIP6 + CESM2-SF + hydroclimate context) and a map-first progression.
- Meeting-informed outline centers the repeated meeting question: seasonal asymmetry of amplified hot-day warming and its mechanism chain.

2. **Research questions are tighter and more explicit**
- Initial questions are broad and include model-observation agreement and forcing attribution early.
- Meeting-informed questions prioritize mechanism sequence first (pattern -> flux repartition -> SM/pathways -> BC decomposition -> framing choice).

3. **Figure logic follows meeting figure evolution**
- Initial figure plan includes strong but heterogeneous candidate sets from project outputs.
- Meeting-informed plan mirrors the figure skeleton used repeatedly in meetings (global pattern, timing, SH/LH diagnosis, conceptual regime, decomposition, framing robustness).

4. **Framing-choice conflict is now explicit**
- Initial outline did not foreground the monthly-median vs annual-mean baseline decision.
- Meeting-informed outline treats this as a core methodological decision to lock before drafting.

5. **Uncertainty discipline increased**
- Initial outline assumes process and attribution layers proceed directly.
- Meeting-informed outline explicitly requires handling unresolved BC offsets and sparse-bin/high-latitude uncertainty.

## Strengths and weaknesses

## Initial outline (`paper/outline.md`)
**Strengths**
- Broad scientific coverage across observations, projections, and forcing attribution.
- Strong connection to existing production figure assets in `/project/...`.
- Good for a comprehensive, data-rich manuscript scope.

**Weaknesses**
- Storyline is less anchored to the human-developed meeting arc.
- May overextend main text by including too many thematic layers at once.
- Under-specifies key narrative decision points (especially anomaly framing and unresolved offsets).

## Meeting-informed outline (`paper/outline_meetings.md`)
**Strengths**
- Closely aligned with the actual development history and advisor-facing presentation logic.
- Clear mechanism chain with explicit checkpoints and decision gates.
- Better separation of robust claims vs unresolved or lower-confidence elements.

**Weaknesses**
- Narrower focus may underplay broader attribution/model-spread scope unless deliberately reincorporated.
- Relies on figure families that include repeated-build slides and occasionally sparse bins, requiring careful curation.
- Needs a deliberate bridge back to the strongest cross-dataset synthesis to avoid appearing too process-local.

## Recommended operating strategy
1. Use the meeting-informed outline as the main manuscript backbone.
2. Pull selected elements from the initial outline (especially model-observation bridge and attribution) only where they directly strengthen the core claim.
3. Keep one anomaly framing primary and place the alternative framing as a structured robustness section.
4. Treat unresolved BC-offset/high-latitude mechanisms as bounded discussion claims, not headline conclusions.
