# p004 paper outline (meeting-informed)

## Scope and intent
This outline prioritizes the narrative and figure logic that repeatedly appeared across the meeting decks (especially 2023-09-27, 2023-11-03, 2024-07-17, 2024-08-13), while using the per-date notes in `notes/meetings/` as supporting evidence.

## Primary research questions (meeting-driven)
1. What is the spatiotemporal pattern of amplified hot-day warming over land (magnitude, sign, season, and regional structure)?
2. Why is amplification strongest during summer in the tropics but often winter/summer-contrast dominated in the extratropics?
3. To what extent can seasonal amplification be explained by surface turbulent-flux repartitioning (`SH`/`LH`) and soil-moisture–state dependence?
4. Which mechanism components dominate (`ΔSM`, Budyko-curve/state changes, pathway structure), and where do residual offsets remain unresolved?
5. Which anomaly framing should be central for the paper narrative: relative to monthly median warming or to annual-mean warming?

## Working central claim
Amplified hot-day warming is a seasonally structured, hydroclimate-regime-dependent phenomenon. A large part of the tropical signal is consistent with turbulent-flux repartitioning and soil-moisture-mediated `LH` changes, but the full response depends on diagnostic framing and includes unresolved components that must be bounded explicitly.

## Recommended paper structure

### 1. Introduction
- Motivate why annual-mean diagnostics miss important extremes-relevant structure.
- Position relative to Byrne (2021), Duan et al., Hsu & Dirmeyer.
- State framing choice and pre-registered robustness (monthly-median vs annual-mean baseline).

### 2. Results I: Phenomenology of amplification
- Global maps: annual mean, seasonal amplitude, annual max/min of amplified hot-day warming.
- Month-of-maximum map and latitude-band percentile-month heatmaps.
- Message: seasonal cycle is comparable to or larger than annual-mean signal in key regions.

### 3. Results II: Surface energy-flux mechanism test
- `ΔSH` and `ΔLH` diagnostics by latitude band and season-percentile bins.
- Message: repartitioning explains much of tropical seasonality, less complete in extratropics/high latitudes.

### 4. Results III: Regime/pathway and decomposition
- Conceptual `LH-SM` regime schematic (water-limited vs energy-limited).
- Budyko/BC decomposition (`Actual`, `Full BC`, `Hist BC`, residual terms).
- Pathway framing and contribution maps/heatmaps.
- Message: `ΔSM`-related components are central; BC/state-change offsets exist and must be handled transparently.

### 5. Results IV: Framing sensitivity and narrative choice
- Direct comparison of anomaly baselines (monthly median vs annual mean).
- Define which framing is primary in main text and why.
- Put alternative framing in robustness/supplement.

### 6. Discussion
- What is robust vs what remains unresolved (especially offset terms and high-latitude behavior).
- Implications for model spread and regional risk interpretation.
- Limits from sparse/hatched bins and possible note/OCR artifacts.
